I was a grown woman, when one day, speaking of Starr King and his love for the White Hills and his loyalty in keeping California in the Union during the Civil War, the woman to whom I was speaking said “Well, he wasn’t a good man.” “Not a good man,” I said. “Why” said she, “You know he was a Universalist.” We have got on a little since that time in toleration, but we need to get on a little more.

My uncles on my mother’s side were great hunters. Foxes and minks and woodchucks were plentiful in those days and a good many of them fell into my uncles’ traps.
